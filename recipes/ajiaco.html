Ajiaco

Ajiaco is one of the many regional Colombian dishes you can’t leave the country without trying. It’s a hearty soup made from three different kinds of potatoes, chicken, guasca leaves, with a half an ear of corn splashed in for good measure. Most times, Ajiaco is accompanied by a plate of rice and a slice of avocado.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast
  • Water
  • Corn
  • Salt and pepper
  • Onion
  • Garlic head
  • Cilantro
  • Potatoes
  • Guasca leaves
  • Rice
  • Avocado

Steps

  1. In a large pot, place the chicken, corn, cilantro, onion, garlic, salt, and pepper. Add the water and bring to a boil, lower the heat to medium, and cook for about 35 to 40 minutes, until the chicken is cooked and tender. Remove the chicken and reserve.
  2. Continue cooking corn for 30 more minutes. Remove the onion and add the potatoes and the guasca leaves. Cook for 30 minutes.
  3. Cut the chicken meat into small pieces and put it back in the pot. Serve the hot Ajiaco with a side dish of white rice and avocado.
